Types of attacks on business information systems

There are lots of different types of cyber attacks carried out on business. Below I will list some of the most popular attacks on business and briefly explain them.

  • Phishing: Phishing is a scam which cyber attackers use by spamming links via email or other online communication methods. Once the victim of the attack clicks on the sent link the attacker will be able gain access to the victims private system or information. This could also be used to attack business and there staff.
  • Denial of Service (DoS): A denial of service or DoS attack is one the oldest types of cyber attacks on business, the method involves overloading a system to slow it down so that the system is not able to function at all or at its original spee. Attackers chose this type o attack to stop the buisness information system untill they receive some sort of ransom.
  • Insider Attacks: An insider attack is when some one working within the business leaks out the security systems to the attacker, who then could go through the system of the business and sell private information that belongs to the business.
  • Malware: A malware arttack is a malicious software, a malicious software downloads onto the computer without the users knowledge, and the software causes damage to the information systems.
  • Password attacks: A password attack is when a attacker has an automated system and spams verious password sequences and combinations untill it gets the right one and has access to your information.
  • Ransomeware: Ransomware is a specific type of malware that gathers and encrypts data and devices on a network, preventing user access. User access is only restored if the hacker’s demands are met, which most often relate to paying some type of ransom

An understanding of the issues around privacy in business information systems?
  • Business information systems provide information that organizations use to manage themselves efficiently and effectively, typically using computer systems and technology.   • The main components of information systems are computer hardware and software, telecommunications, databases and data warehouses, human resources, and procedures.
